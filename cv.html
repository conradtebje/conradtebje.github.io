Relevant Experience

Major Project: Phase 1 Year 3

Old Portsmouth Masterplan - Urban Strategy

A team-led masterplanning exercise for Old Portsmouth in which I took a leadership role - interpreting the brief, organising workflow, delegating tasks based on individual strengths, and guiding key design decisions across a site encompassing the entirety of Old Portsmouth.

The proposal introduced selective removals - the SubseaCraft office building and the Wightlink terminal - to release the waterfront for public use. Interventions were positioned to avoid encroachment on the historic context, instead establishing a new social focus around Portsmouth Point.

The masterplan proposed a research centre, education facility, gallery, pavilion, market space, social hub, and residential zone, supported by improved pedestrian connections and public realm. This phase strengthened my ability to coordinate large-scale urban analysis within a team.

Major Project: Phase 2 Year 3

The Berm / Meridian Building - Mixed-Use Civic Maritime Hub, Old Portsmouth

Working solo, I developed a mixed-use civic building on a reclaimed waterfront site at The Hard, Portsmouth, designed to give back more than it takes. Three marine environmental charities - Blue Marine Foundation, the Marine Conservation Society, and Hampshire & Isle of Wight Wildlife Trust - occupy public galleries alongside a shared research workshop, wet lab, and internal dock.

The building includes offices, residential apartments, a restaurant, and a cafe. The berm roof is a continuous accessible green surface connecting the public realm to the waterfront. The project was developed through RIBA Stages 0–3, covering site analysis, concept, spatial planning, material strategy, and technical drawings.

Materials were chosen for context and performance: GGBS concrete to reduce embodied carbon, corten steel in reference to the dockyard, and timber throughout the public interior.
